About Me

I’m Mimi, (currently) a computer programming student and (forever) a lifelong learner.

My experience lies in working with object-oriented programming languages like Java, Kotlin, and C# as well as web development languages and tools like HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Bootstrap. I first started in programming in my junior year of high school, where I challenged myself to an introductory college-level course in Java. I loved the idea of being capable of conveying the impossible and bringing something new to life through a computer. My appreciation for the career continued to grow over the years, and I ultimately decided on software development as my lifelong passion. My current learning endeavors follow Linux and its terminal, mobile application development, and computer hardware.

I attend Southeast Technical Institute in Sioux Falls, South Dakota as a soon-to-be 2020 graduate with support from the Build Dakota Scholarship, a full-ride scholarship for in-demand tech careers. Upon graduating with an associate’s degree in computer programming, I have plans to continue my education and aim for a bachelor’s degree.

As a soon-to-be college graduate, I am currently seeking software development internships and full-time opportunities in the Sioux Falls, South Dakota area. Please contact me if you are interested in getting in touch!
